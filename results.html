Highlights

What We Accomplished

A New Mathematical Theory for Complex Networks

Developed the first low-dimensional theory describing emergent behavior in complex networks of nonlinear dynamical systems over finite time scales — filling a critical gap that standard tools could not address. Published in Physical Review X (2020).

Theory Network Science Physical Review X
Read paper

Predicting Critical Transitions from Data

Demonstrated for the first time that critical transitions in complex networks can be predicted directly from data — without needing to know the underlying equations. Opens the door to early-warning systems for ecological collapses, financial crises, and brain seizures.

Critical Transitions Data-Driven Early Warning
Publications

COVID-19: Robot Dance & Vaccine Strategy

Applied network theory to the COVID-19 pandemic. The Robot Dance optimization platform guided city-level intervention policies across Brazil. A PNAS paper on delayed vaccine doses directly influenced national vaccination strategies, and a Lancet study provided key epidemiological data for Rio de Janeiro.

COVID-19 PNAS The Lancet Public Health
Read paper

Emergent Hypernetworks — Nature Communications

Discovered that weakly coupled oscillators spontaneously form hypernetworks — higher-order interaction structures that emerge from the dynamics themselves. Published in Nature Communications (2022), with a second Nature Communications paper on coherence resonance in influencer networks (2021).

The Royal Society Prize & International Recognition

PI Tiago Pereira received the prestigious Newton Advanced Fellowship from The Royal Society, recognising the project's contributions to mathematical sciences. The group also received the Serrapilheira Institute grant, one of the most competitive private science grants in Brazil.

Training the Next Generation

Mentored over 20 PhD students, postdocs, and visiting researchers from Brazil and abroad. Alumni are now active at institutions including Imperial College London, KHAS University, and across Brazilian universities. Reading groups, workshops and seminars ran continuously from 2018 to 2024.

What Comes Next

Open Directions

Sparse Network Reconstruction

The 2023 arXiv paper on robust reconstruction of sparse network dynamics opens a clear avenue for a new funded project applying these methods to real-world biological and infrastructure networks.

AI + Dynamical Systems

Combining the group's data-driven reconstruction methods with modern machine learning frameworks — particularly for predicting tipping points in climate and ecological systems.

Smart Cities & Infrastructure

Applying the critical transition theory to urban sensor networks. The mathematical tools are ready; what's needed is a partnership with city-scale data providers and applied funding.
