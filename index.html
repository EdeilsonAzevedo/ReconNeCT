Core Research Areas

What We Investigate

01

Network Reconstruction

Inferring the hidden topology of complex systems from observable time-series data using novel statistical methods.

02

Critical Transitions

Identifying early-warning signals that precede abrupt tipping points in ecological, financial, and social systems.

03

Rule Inference

Extracting governing equations and interaction rules of dynamical systems directly from empirical observations.

04

Temporal Dynamics

Modeling how networks evolve in time and how structural changes influence system-wide dynamics and resilience.

05

Resilience Analysis

Quantifying the robustness of interconnected systems and designing interventions to prevent catastrophic collapse.

06

Data-Driven Methods

Developing machine learning and statistical frameworks tailored to sparse, noisy, high-dimensional network data.
