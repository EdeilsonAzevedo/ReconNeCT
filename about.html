The Project

ReconNeCT

"Join us in the tour to uncover the rules of complex systems from data and to predict the sudden changes of their behavior."

We aim at reconstructing the connectivity networks from data and predicting the sudden changes of their behavior — developing strategies to avoid malfunctioning in complex systems.

The first step is to develop a mathematical theory to describe emergent behavior in complex networks of nonlinear dynamical systems. These complex systems have wild global behavior making relevant phenomena such as collective dynamics take place over finite time scales — and cannot be tackled by standard tools.

The ReconNeCT Project fills this gap by developing a theory for emergent phenomena in complex networks. This theory opens enormous possibilities such as predicting critical transitions and reconstructing network dynamics from data.

Two Core Objectives

  1. For each connectivity level, this theory will provide a low-dimensional description over finite time scales — allowing for a description of collective phenomena in terms of the network structure.
  2. The above objectives provide effect models over connectivity layers, offering a way to reverse engineer the problem and obtain a model from data — opening the possibility to predict critical transitions from data which would be impossible otherwise.

We apply this theory to neuron networks and smart cities sensor data to predict critical transitions and help preventing catastrophes.
